Dropsy

Symptoms

- Bloating of the body, protruding scales.

- Dropsy is caused from a bacterial infection (acromonas) of the kidneys, causing fluid accumulation or renal failure.

- The fluids in the body build up and cause the fish to bloat up and the scales to protrude.

Treatment

- An effective treatment is to add an antibiotic to the food.

- With flake food, use about 1% of antibiotic and carefully mix it.

- Antibiotics in 250 mg capsules if added to 25 grams of flake food will be sufficient to treat dozens of fishes.

- A good antibiotic is Chloromycetin (chloramphenicol), or use tetracycline.
